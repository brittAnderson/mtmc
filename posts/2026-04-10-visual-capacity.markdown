# Neuroscience Doesn't Do Theories

The title: "What is the visual capacity of the post-mortem brain?" comes from Mario Senden in his [video chat](https://www.thetransmitter.org/methods/what-a-birds-eye-view-of-half-a-million-papers-reveals-about-neuroscience/) with Mac Shine available on [The Transmitter](https://www.thetransmitter.org). This is a fascinating and accessible chat discussing how Senden looked for structure in the research of neuroscientists via a bibliographic analysis. Most pertinent was a quote in the accompanying text: "Not a single cluster in the entire 175-cluster solution is organized around a theoretical framework. The Bayesian brain, the free energy principle and predictive coding are common targets of empirical science, yet none of them anchor their own research community. Theory, it seems, is something neuroscience does around the edges of the phenomena it is really interested in."

Do we really think the deep insights into mental mechanisms that many of us crave will come from atheoretical puzzle solving?

"From this I reach what I might call a philosophy; at any rate it is a constant idea of mine; that behind the cotton wool is hidden a pattern; that we—I mean all human beings—are connected with this;" -- Virginia Woolf, [Moments of Being (p 17)](https://archive.org/details/momentsofbeing00wool)
